We built Ailurus for creators who want to own their audience and their payout path. Today, centralized platforms take large cuts, control your files, and can remove you overnight. Web3 alternatives ask fans for wallets, gas fees, and seed phrases. That is not mainstream ready. Ailurus targets a proven subscription model on Sui Testnet with Google login, USDC memberships, Walrus storage, and Seal subscription walls.

Here is the live product on Testnet. The landing page states our pitch: publish privately and get paid globally. Fans can enter with Google through Enoki zkLogin. Creators price memberships in USDC while fans never see gas. I open the live demo feed to browse posts from on-chain creators. The interface feels like a familiar social feed, not a wallet dashboard. On Explore we discover creator profiles registered through our Move contract on Sui. Subscriber-only posts remain encrypted until a fan pays in USDC. When a fan subscribes, the Move contract records subscription state on chain. Behind the interface, media bytes live on Walrus and Seal enforces decrypt access only for active subscribers. Creators can upload encrypted drops through the Walrus SDK pipeline. Our Cloudflare worker sponsors gas through Enoki with an allowlist of Move targets. This is identity, settlement, storage, and privacy working together on one product path. The full stack is deployed on Sui Testnet today.

In summary, Ailurus is a Web2 grade creator subscription layer on the full Sui stack. The GitHub repo is public, contracts are live on Testnet, and we are targeting Mainnet in July twenty twenty six.
